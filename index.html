Posted by: Kelly McGonigal "Willpower"

Willpower taught at Stanford! This course is incredibly popular among students - everyone wants to strengthen willpower. But so far there has not been a teacher who would undertake to take a science-based course on this topic, dig up other people's research and conduct his own. Everything to prove that we train this skill and teach everyone who wants it to develop.
This teacher was a psychologist, Ph.D. Kelly McGonigal - who subsequently wrote The Willpower Instinct, which we published under the title Willpower. It was she who brought the idea of ​​the will-training course to the campus of business school No. 1 in the world, developed it and continues to this day, receiving gratitude from students and many teaching awards.
What caught us in the book:
It turned out that willpower can be trained like muscles. The more you train, the easier it is to use. The position “I work on the task for 10 minutes and then I can quit”, oddly enough, greatly increases the chances of completing the case. Sleep and rest in the most direct way strengthen willpower, and stress reduces it. The fact is that a tense organism does its best to direct us to pleasure, even dubious in terms of long-term goals.

"Mind power into 21 century by John Kehoe"

In Mind Power Into the 21st Century, John Kehoe has articulated a set of life-changing principles for charting a course to success and happiness.
More than that, Mind Power Into the 21st Century presents a remarkably specific and practical guide that shows you how to:
* Harness the Powers of your Subconscious Mind
* Tap into your Creativity
* Successfully use Visualization Techniques
* Tap into your Intuition
* Interpret your Dreams
* Heal Yourself
* Develop a "Prosperity Consciousness"
* Build and Maintain Fulfilling Relationships and much more
Mind Power is without a doubt the best self-help, motivational, goal-setting book. Chapters are short and to the point, giving the reader only the meat.
Kehoe uses language that is plain and easy to understand - making it a breeze to take in the information. There are no theoretical ramblings, only first
hand examples and procedures.

Posted by: Kitami Macao
"Samurai without a sword"

Japanese wisdom and philosophy are particularly attractive; many people in European countries turn to Eastern teachings in difficult times. The book "Samurai without a sword", written by Kitami Masao, tells about the philosophy of the famous historical figure of Japan of the 16th century Toyotomi Hideyoshi. It is written as if he himself shares his thoughts, which allows him to be even more inspired by his ideas. The narrative is more like a memoir and an autobiography than a textbook, but it contains a lot of useful information.
This book will benefit those who want to achieve their goals, who seek to become a leader. Although Toyotomi Hideyoshi lived in the 16th century, his advice remains relevant in the 21st century. At a time when in Japan power was transferred only through the bloodline, and Toyotomi was from a simple peasant family, he was able to achieve a high post in society.
He became a leader and was able to unite a country that was in a difficult situation at that time. Toyotomi became a samurai, as he dreamed of as a child, only he defended his beliefs and the interests of his country not with the help of the sword, but with the power of reason, his wisdom. That is why the book has such a name.
The author tells how to behave in difficult situations, how to build relationships with colleagues and motivate subordinates. The book provides food for thought and information on how to learn to solve all issues peacefully, thanks to wisdom and a special type of thinking.
